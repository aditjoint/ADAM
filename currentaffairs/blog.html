4. Sectoral Risks and the Need for Autonomy

4.1 Economic Security and Continuity

Global Risk: Dependence on U.S.-controlled platforms such as AWS or Microsoft Azure introduces operational risks during sanctions.

Indian Advantage: Local software stacks and data centers ensure seamless continuity even amid global disruptions.

4.2 SWIFT & Payment Dependencies

Global Risk: Financial institutions risk exclusion from SWIFT and dollar settlements, affecting payments via Stripe, Visa, or PayPal.

Indian Resilience: UPI and RuPay, integrated with domestic banking systems, ensure financial autonomy.

4.3 Export Tariffs & MSME Pressures

Global Risk: MSMEs reliant on imported SaaS, ERP, or cloud services are highly exposed to licensing or cost disruptions.

Indian Solution: Indigenous ERP, CRM, and hosting solutions prevent such disruptions and offer sustainable pricing.

4.4 Technology Sovereignty

Global Risk: Export bans on chips, AI models, or APIs can halt innovation pipelines.

Indian Advantage: Investing in chip design, Indian SaaS, and open-source stacks preserves innovation independence.

4.5 Geopolitical Insurance

Global Risk: Clashes with Western strategic interests may expose Indian firms to sudden sanctions.

Indian Advantage: Choosing Indian tech safeguards national economic interest and aligns with Atmanirbhar Bharat goals.

4.6 MSME & Employment Resilience

Global Risk: Rising import costs and supply chain disruptions can lead to shutdowns and job losses.

Indian Advantage: Local solutions keep employment and skills within the country, bolstering economic resilience.
