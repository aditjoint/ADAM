Whistleblower Policy (India)

Effective Date: April 15, 2025

1. Purpose

This policy is framed in accordance with the provisions of Section 177(9) of the Companies Act, 2013, and the rules made thereunder. It aims to provide a mechanism for employees and stakeholders to report unethical behavior, actual or suspected fraud, or violations of company policies.

2. Applicability

This policy applies to all employees, directors, vendors, consultants, interns, and stakeholders of ADAM Consult, across all locations in India.

3. What Constitutes Whistleblowing?

Concerns that may be reported under this policy include but are not limited to:

  • Violation of any law or regulation (including tax, anti-corruption, or environmental laws)
  • Accounting or auditing irregularities
  • Misuse or misappropriation of company assets
  • Bribery, corruption, or kickbacks
  • Sexual harassment or workplace misconduct
  • Endangering public health or safety
  • Concealment of any of the above

Reports can be made anonymously or with full identity disclosure. The whistleblower may choose to report through:

5. Investigation and Confidentiality

All complaints will be acknowledged and investigated by a designated Whistleblower Committee. The identity of the complainant will be kept confidential unless disclosure is required by law.

6. Protection Against Retaliation

ADAM Consult is committed to ensuring that no employee or stakeholder who makes a disclosure in good faith will be harassed, retaliated against, or subjected to unfair treatment. Any reprisal will lead to disciplinary action.

7. False Allegations

Whistleblowers must ensure that their allegations are factual and not motivated by personal grievances. False or malicious complaints will be treated as misconduct and dealt with under the disciplinary policy.

8. Regulatory Compliance

This policy aligns with:

  • Companies Act, 2013 (Section 177)
  • SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015 – for listed entities
  • Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013

9. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the management and board, and may be updated based on regulatory changes or operational needs.
